Work

Eyesore Media

Eyesore Media is a film and television focused review blog. This blog is a passion project for my client as they have a love for provocative stories (hence the name). The client plans to mainly cover Horror, Drama and Thriller titles.

The client wanted a design to reflect the mood of these genres of film, be clean, and minimal. They wanted the blog to have two main elements the content (review/opinion pieces) and screenshots from the titles.

For the design I was inspired by movie posters and how they incorporated the title picturesquely. With the client wanting screenshots to be a major visual element, I decide on styling the post links as mini movie posters. With Screen Media being a visual medium, I added motion to the website mainly as user interaction notification.

This website represents a culmination of all I have learnt in Front-end and Design, from my past two years learning Web Development at TAFE. My goal with this website was to make it as light and independent as possible. Outside of Foundation for the layout, I crafted my own modular JavaScript and CSS with pre-processed SCSS.

Boom Radio

As a part of my Diploma of Website Development I was put in a team with three other students. My team was tasked with planning, designing, and creating a website for a real-life client. The client project my team was task with was Boom Radio, a radio station run by the students of Leederville TAFE.

The Boom Radio website is a post driven platform, that allows the various students to be able to create posts and additional pages. The website also includes a radio player featured on every page that lets users listen to the student’s radio shows while they browse the site. The client wanted the design to be themed around comic books.

My team used WordPress to implement the CMS and customised the backend to allow for proper categorising of posts. Our client’s previous website had an issue with their radio player, where it would stop playing when navigating to different pages.

This was a big usability issue for users and something my team wanted to rectify in this new website. To accomplish this my team had to restructure the website into a single-page application. This allowed the radio player to continue to play when navigating to different pages.

The Boom Radio project was yearlong endeavour between designing, coding, and project management. My group faced challenges ranging from technical issues, team members dropping out, gaining a new member, and working from home. We as a team overcame these issues through individual hard work, teamwork, and good communication skills.
